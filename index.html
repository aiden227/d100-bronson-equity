Frequently Asked Questions

What is a real estate syndication?

A syndication is a way for investors to pool their capital together to purchase a large property (like a 200-unit apartment building). You invest as a limited partner, and the general partners (our team) handle everything: finding the deal, managing the property, and distributing returns to investors.

Do I need to be an accredited investor?

Most of our deals are structured under SEC Rule 506(b), which allows both accredited and sophisticated investors. On your introductory call, we will discuss your specific situation and what opportunities are available to you.

What is the minimum investment?

The minimum is typically $50,000, though some investors choose to start with more. Our Recessionary Income Fund has a $100,000 minimum.

How often do I receive distributions?

Most of our real estate syndications distribute quarterly. Our Recessionary Income Fund distributes monthly. Distributions typically begin 6-9 months after the deal closes.

What kind of returns can I expect?

Returns vary by deal and asset class. Our exited multifamily deals have returned between 14% and 100% IRR. Our oil and gas partnerships target 25-35% annual returns. Our debt fund targets 7-14% projected annual returns. Past performance on realized investments is not indicative of future results. Projected returns are not guaranteed. All investments carry risk.

What tax benefits are available?

Real estate investments provide depreciation and cost segregation benefits that can offset W-2 income. Oil and gas partnerships can provide 75-90% first-year tax deductions. Consult your tax professional for your specific situation.

How do you vet the deals?

Every deal is personally reviewed by Bronson Hill and our advisory board, which includes Ken McElroy ($1B+ AUM), Michael Blank (2,000+ units), and Drew Whitson (finance professor, GP in 2,800+ units). We underwrite conservatively and build in margin of safety.
